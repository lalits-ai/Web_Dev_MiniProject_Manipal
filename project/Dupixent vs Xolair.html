Comparing Dupixent vs Fasenra

Dupixent (dupilumab)

Dupixent is an injection that is usually given under the skin every other week for the treatment of asthma, eczema, and some other inflammatory conditions. Injection site reactions and eye conditions are the most common side effects reported and, unlike several other biologics, the risk of infection is low.

Prescribed for Eosinophilic Esophagitis, Prurigo Nodularis, Atopic Dermatitis, Chronic Rhinosinusitis with Nasal Polyps, Eczema, Asthma - Maintenance.

Dupixent may also be used for purposes not listed in this comparison guide.

    Fasenra

    Fasenra is a biologic that works by blocking the action of IL-5, which is the main cytokine responsible for the survival and production of eosinophils. Fasenra may be used to treat severe eosinophilic asthma and, after an initial loading dose, may be self-administered every eight weeks using a Fasenra autoinjector pen. Common side effects include headache, fever, pharyngitis, and injection site reactions.

    Prescribed for Asthma.
